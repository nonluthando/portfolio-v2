SeaClear

Human-computer interaction capstone focused on making beach water quality information easier to access, understand and act on.

Problem

Beachgoers need clear information about water quality, but environmental health data can be difficult to interpret. A system that simply displays data is not enough. Users need navigation, context, explanations and feedback that help them make safer decisions.

What I built

SeaClear is a group-built HCI platform for Cape Town beach water quality information. It includes beach information, maps, news, educational content, community reporting and admin-related workflows.

My contribution

  • Improved homepage layout, beach cards, map placement and carousel structure.
  • Designed and structured News and Education pages.
  • Created content around waterborne illness, public health risks and economic impact.
  • Built the user-facing community report flow with form handling and beach selection.
  • Improved navigation, flash messages and information structure.
  • Contributed test cases, documentation, report sections and a user manual.

Technical implementation

SeaClear used Flask with template rendering and MongoDB-backed data flows. My work focused on user-facing templates, form flow, content structure and usability-driven page improvements. The community reporting feature connected user input to backend handling so submitted reports could be saved.

Engineering decisions

  • Prioritised clarity over visual complexity because the system communicates public health information.
  • Used heuristic-guided iteration to improve navigation and reduce user confusion.
  • Structured educational content into practical sections instead of dense text.
  • Kept community reporting simple to make participation less intimidating.

Testing and validation

I authored test cases for key user flows and contributed to documentation. HCI validation focused on navigation clarity, feedback, information structure and the usability of user-facing flows.

What I learned

SeaClear strengthened my ability to translate HCI principles into concrete implementation decisions. I learned that usability is not separate from engineering. It affects routes, forms, validation, feedback and how content is structured.

Future improvements

Future improvements include live data integration, stronger map interactions, deeper accessibility testing and more structured moderation workflows for community reports.

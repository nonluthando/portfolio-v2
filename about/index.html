About

Systems, numbers, and people.

That combination explains most of what I enjoy building.

I studied Computer Science and Psychology at the University of Cape Town, with additional Applied Statistics coursework. I like software that has structure behind it and a clear reason to exist.

My route into software

Computer Science gave me the technical base. I built strength in programming, algorithms, databases, networks, operating systems, and software engineering. Psychology shaped how I think about people using the systems we build. Applied Statistics added another layer. It made me more comfortable reasoning with data, uncertainty, and evidence.

Together, those areas made me especially interested in products that sit between data and decision-making. I like systems that help people understand something, choose something, or act with more confidence.

What I value in a build

Clarity The logic should make sense and the user should not be left guessing.
Reliability Tests, validation, and predictable behaviour matter.
Honesty A product should make clear what it knows, what it assumes, and where its limits are.
Usefulness I want to build things that solve a real friction point rather than just showcase a stack.

Commercial experience

I worked as a software engineering contractor in an EdTech startup environment. The work moved across backend features, product logic, NLP functionality, and production debugging.

Backend and product work

I contributed across Laravel and Flask services, worked with database-backed functionality, and dealt with the sort of issues that appear in real teams: failing tests, awkward edge cases, dependency problems, and things that work locally but break in CI.

NLP exposure

I also worked with spaCy NER workflows and related product work. That gave me hands-on exposure to the practical side of AI-enabled software, where model behaviour, integration, and product context all matter.

What kinds of roles suit me

Backend engineering

APIs, product logic, database design, testing, and deployment are the most natural fit for me right now.

Data-oriented product work

I am drawn to products where decisions, scoring, analytics, or structured information play a central role.

AI-enabled systems

Especially where AI is part of a bigger product and has to live inside real workflows, not just a demo.

A few things that matter to me

I like thoughtful teams. I like direct feedback. I like work that asks both technical and product questions. I also care a lot about whether a system respects the person using it. That shows up in my projects again and again.

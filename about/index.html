About Me

Backend · Systems · Accessibility · Data-Driven Applications

I approach software as both a technical and human problem, focusing on building reliable systems that are understandable, maintainable, and genuinely useful to the people who rely on them.

My Approach to Software

Combining technical reliability with people-centred thinking.

I care about building systems that are reliable, predictable, and thoughtfully designed. My work is shaped by the belief that good software should reduce friction rather than create it, especially for users operating under different levels of confidence, technical familiarity, or real-world constraints.

I am especially interested in maintainable backend systems with clear structure, understandable behaviour, and clean interfaces. I value thoughtful validation, reliable workflows, and code that can be tested, understood, and evolved over time.

For me, good system design is not only about correctness, but also about clarity, usability, and supporting confidence for both users and future developers.

Accessibility & Human-Centred Thinking

Designing systems with clarity, usability, and inclusion in mind.

Accessibility matters to me because it shapes who gets to benefit from technology. Through my academic background and project work, I became increasingly aware of how design decisions affect learnability, confidence, and long-term engagement.

I am drawn to clarity-first design, meaningful feedback, and systems that guide users rather than overwhelm them. This perspective influences how I think about backend validation, information structure, error handling, and user flows.

My background in both Computer Science and Psychology strengthened my ability to think about software not only as a technical system, but also as an experience shaped by human behaviour, communication, and accessibility.

Continuous Learning & Technical Growth

Building beyond coursework through self-directed technical exploration.

Much of my technical growth has come from independently exploring systems, tools, and engineering concepts beyond formal coursework.

I enjoy challenging myself with projects that require learning unfamiliar technologies, debugging complex behaviour, and thinking carefully about maintainability, scalability, and system structure.

This mindset led me to explore backend engineering, concurrency, deployment workflows, data-oriented applications, CI/CD pipelines, and production-focused software development through self-directed projects and experimentation.

I value adaptability and enjoy environments where I can continue learning while contributing meaningfully to technically challenging work.

Leadership & Community Involvement

Experiences that strengthened collaboration, communication, and people-focused problem solving.

Alongside my technical work, I have been involved in leadership, tutoring, and student-facing roles that strengthened my ability to work with people, communicate clearly, and contribute within teams.

I volunteered with the University of Cape Town Science Students’ Council as part of the Events Committee, where I helped support student-facing events and collaborative initiatives. This strengthened my organisational skills and ability to work effectively in structured teams.

I also contributed to event coordination at MyDomain Residence, which reinforced the importance of listening to people’s needs, improving participation, and maintaining smooth execution under practical constraints.

In addition, I tutored first-year students during their transition into university. This experience deepened my understanding of the digital divide, particularly how thoughtful structure, guidance, and supportive feedback can meaningfully improve confidence and accessibility.

Looking Ahead

Areas I am actively growing toward.

I am motivated by opportunities where I can continue learning, contribute meaningfully, and build systems that are both technically sound and considerate of the people who use them.

I am particularly interested in backend engineering, data-oriented systems, and roles that value strong fundamentals, adaptability, analytical thinking, and clear problem solving.

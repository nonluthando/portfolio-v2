About Me

Systems, numbers and people.

Those are the three things I keep coming back to in my work.

I hold a BSc in Computer Science and Psychology from the University of Cape Town, with additional coursework in Applied Statistics. That combination affects the problems I notice, the questions I ask and the way I explain technical decisions.

How I Think About Software

I usually start by trying to understand what the system is meant to make easier.

I am interested in what information enters a system, how it changes, where decisions are made and what can go wrong along the way. I also care about whether the final result is useful to the person receiving it.

My strongest work sits where backend logic, data and human behaviour meet. I value clear boundaries, predictable behaviour, good validation, explainable outputs and code that can be tested as the system changes.

Psychology and HCI help me think about friction, confidence, accessibility and communication. Applied Statistics coursework strengthened how I think about evidence, uncertainty and evaluation.

Commercial Growth

My contract role gave me experience working inside an existing codebase rather than starting from a blank project.

I worked across Laravel/PHP and Python/Flask services on database-driven features, API integrations, tests, CI workflows, dependency problems and deployment debugging.

I also worked on spaCy NER functionality, including data preparation, entity classification, pipeline changes, tests and inference integration. That experience taught me to treat NLP as part of the wider system, with the same need for testing, clear interfaces and maintainable behaviour.

Continuous Learning

I learn best by building, tracing behaviour and improving the system in small steps.

Most of my formal Computer Science coursework used Java. I expanded into Python backend development through personal projects and commercial work, then into FastAPI, Flask, Laravel, PostgreSQL, testing, CI/CD, NLP workflows and production debugging.

I am now building a Django application for interview preparation. The project is giving me room to work more deeply with domain modelling, authentication, test-driven development, review history and deterministic planning.

What I Am Looking For

Graduate and junior roles where I can contribute to real systems and keep growing.

I am targeting software engineering roles with a focus on backend development, applied NLP or data-oriented systems. I am most interested in teams that value strong fundamentals, clear thinking, curiosity and steady ownership of more complex work over time.
